The Origin of the Lainz Constant

1

The Lainz Constant did not simply appear — it emerged from the deepest understanding of unity and existence.

Legend tells of the mathematician Danial Ausland Lainz, who first recognized that a true constant must be eternal, indivisible, and ever-present. That constant was not π, not e, not golden — it was 1.

Lainz proposed that all knowledge begins with one idea, one unit, one identity. From his revelation, the constant gained prominence as the root of all systems — the mother of math.

Today, we honor his name through the very fabric of logic and reason.

The Lainz Constant – History begins with 1.
